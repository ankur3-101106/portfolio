## Lecture 31: <br />Generalised ElGamal Public Key Cryptosystem
:::

## Setup (General)

* Cyclic group:
  $$
  G
  $$

* Generator:
  $$
  \alpha
  $$

* Private key:
  $$
  a
  $$

* Public key:
  $$
  \beta = \alpha^a
  $$

* Public:
  $$
  (G,\alpha,\beta)
  $$

---

## Encryption

Choose random:
$$
k
$$

Ciphertext:
$$
y_1 = \alpha^k
$$

$$
y_2 = x \cdot \beta^k
$$

---

## Decryption

$$
x = y_2 \cdot (y_1^a)^{-1}
$$

---

## Core Hard Problem

$$
\beta = \alpha^a \Rightarrow \text{find } a
$$

(Discrete Log Problem)

---

## Elliptic Curve Version

* Group:
  $$
  E(\mathbb{Z}_p)
  $$

* Operation: point addition

---

## Keys

* Generator point:
  $$
  \alpha
  $$

* Private key:
  $$
  a
  $$

* Public key:
  $$
  \beta = a\alpha
  $$

---

## Encryption (ECC)

Choose random:
$$
k
$$

$$
y_1 = k\alpha
$$

$$
y_2 = x + k\beta
$$

---

## Decryption

$$
x = y_2 - a y_1
$$

---

## Example Values

* $p = 11$

* Curve:
  $$
  y^2 \equiv x^3 + x + 6 \pmod{11}
  $$

* Generator:
  $$
  \alpha = (2,7)
  $$

* Private:
  $$
  a = 7
  $$

* Public:
  $$
  \beta = (7,2)
  $$

---

## Encryption Example

Message:
$$
x = (10,9)
$$

Random:
$$
k = 3
$$

$$
y_1 = (8,3)
$$

$$
y_2 = (10,2)
$$

---

## Decryption

$$
x = (10,2) - 7(8,3) = (10,9)
$$

---

## Notes

* Uses point addition instead of multiplication

* Security based on:
  $$
  \text{ECDLP}
  $$

* Smaller keys, strong security

## Lecture 32: <br />Chinese Remainder Theorem
:::

## Linear Equations (mod p)

Given:
$$
4x + 7y \equiv 9 \pmod{13}
$$
$$
5x + 2y \equiv 3 \pmod{13}
$$

---

### Steps

* Multiply by inverses:

$$
4^{-1} \equiv 10 \pmod{13}
$$
$$
5^{-1} \equiv 8 \pmod{13}
$$

---

### Transform

$$
x + 5y \equiv 12 \pmod{13}
$$
$$
x + 3y \equiv 11 \pmod{13}
$$

---

### Subtract

$$
2y \equiv 1 \pmod{13}
$$

$$
y \equiv 7 \pmod{13}
$$

---

### Substitute

$$
x = 12 - 5y \equiv 3 \pmod{13}
$$

---

## Solution

$$
x = 3,\quad y = 7
$$

---

## Chinese Remainder Theorem (2 equations)

Given:
$$
x \equiv a \pmod{m}
$$
$$
x \equiv b \pmod{n}
$$

Condition:
$$
\gcd(m,n)=1
$$

---

## General Form

$$
x = a + tm
$$

Substitute:

$$
a + tm \equiv b \pmod{n}
$$

---

### Solve for ( t )

$$
t \equiv m^{-1}(b-a) \pmod{n}
$$

---

### Final Solution

$$
x = a + tm \pmod{mn}
$$

---

## Example

$$
x \equiv 3 \pmod{5}
$$
$$
x \equiv 5 \pmod{7}
$$

---

### Compute

$$
5^{-1} \equiv 3 \pmod{7}
$$

$$
t = 3(5-3) \equiv 6 \pmod{7}
$$

---

### Result

$$
x = 3 + 5 \cdot 6 = 33
$$

$$
x \equiv 33 \pmod{35}
$$

---

## General CRT (r equations)

Given:
$$
x \equiv a_i \pmod{m_i}
$$

Condition:
$$
\gcd(m_i,m_j)=1
$$

---

### Define

$$
M = \prod m_i
$$

$$
M_i = \frac{M}{m_i}
$$

$$
y_i = M_i^{-1} \pmod{m_i}
$$

---

### Solution

$$
x = \sum a_i M_i y_i \pmod{M}
$$

---

## Key Properties

* Unique solution:
  $$
  \mod \left(\prod m_i\right)
  $$

* Works only if moduli are pairwise coprime

---

## Key Insight

* Break big modulus into smaller ones
* Solve separately
* Combine using CRT

---

## Final Note

* Used in:

  * RSA optimization
  * Cryptographic computations

## Lecture 33: <br />Rabin Cryptosystem
:::

## Quadratic Residue

Definition:

$$
a \text{ is quadratic residue mod } p \iff \exists y:\ y^2 \equiv a \pmod{p}
$$

---

## Euler Criterion

$$
a^{\frac{p-1}{2}} \equiv 1 \pmod{p} ;; \Rightarrow ;; a \text{ is QR}
$$

$$
a^{\frac{p-1}{2}} \equiv -1 \pmod{p} ;; \Rightarrow ;; a \text{ is non-QR}
$$

---

## Square Root (Special Case)

If:

$$
p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}
$$

Then:

$$
y \equiv \pm a^{\frac{p+1}{4}} \pmod{p}
$$

---

## Key Generation

* Choose primes:

$$
p \equiv q \equiv 3 \pmod{4}
$$

* Compute:

$$
n = pq
$$

* Public key:

$$
n
$$

* Private key:

$$
(p, q)
$$

---

## Plaintext Space

$$
x \in \mathbb{Z}_n^*
$$

---

## Encryption

$$
y \equiv x^2 \pmod{n}
$$

---

## Decryption

Solve:

$$
x^2 \equiv y \pmod{n}
$$

Using CRT:

$$
x^2 \equiv y \pmod{p}
$$

$$
x^2 \equiv y \pmod{q}
$$

---

## Roots

From each:

$$
x \equiv \pm y^{\frac{p+1}{4}} \pmod{p}
$$

$$
x \equiv \pm y^{\frac{q+1}{4}} \pmod{q}
$$

---

## Total Solutions

$$
4 \text{ possible values of } x
$$

---

## Example

Given:

$$
p = 7,; q = 11,; n = 77
$$

Ciphertext:

$$
y = 23
$$

---

## Solve

$$
x^2 \equiv 23 \pmod{7}
$$

$$
x^2 \equiv 23 \pmod{11}
$$

---

## Final Solutions

$$
x \in {10, 32, 45, 67}
$$

---

## Key Points

* Not injective → 1 ciphertext → 4 plaintexts
* Requires guessing / redundancy
* Based on square root modulo problem

---

## Insight

* Uses:

  * CRT
  * Quadratic residues
  * Modular square roots

* Limitation:

  * Ambiguity (4 solutions) → not widely used 

:::info[TITLE]
## Lecture 34: <br />Legendre and Jacobi Symbol
:::

## Quadratic Residues Count

* In ( $$\mathbb{Z}_p$$ ) (p prime):

$$
|\text{QR}| = |\text{Non-QR}| = \frac{p-1}{2}
$$

---

## Construction

Set:
$$
{1^2, 2^2, ..., (\frac{p-1}{2})^2} \mod p
$$

✔ Gives all quadratic residues

---

## Legendre Symbol

$$
\left(\frac{a}{p}\right) =
\begin{cases}
0 & p \mid a \
1 & a \text{ is QR mod } p \
-1 & a \text{ is non-QR mod } p
\end{cases}
$$

---

## Euler Relation

$$
\left(\frac{a}{p}\right) \equiv a^{\frac{p-1}{2}} \mod p
$$

---

## Multiplicative Property

$$
\left(\frac{mn}{p}\right) = \left(\frac{m}{p}\right)\left(\frac{n}{p}\right)
$$

---

## Special Values

### For ( -1 )

$$
\left(\frac{-1}{p}\right) = (-1)^{\frac{p-1}{2}}
$$

* (= 1) if:
  $$
  p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}
  $$

* (= -1) if:
  $$
  p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}
  $$

---

### For ( 2 )

$$
\left(\frac{2}{p}\right) = (-1)^{\frac{p^2 - 1}{8}}
$$

* (= 1) if:
  $$
  p \equiv \pm 1 \pmod{8}
  $$

* (= -1) if:
  $$
  p \equiv \pm 3 \pmod{8}
  $$

---

## Congruence Property

If:
$$
a \equiv b \pmod{p}
$$

Then:
$$
\left(\frac{a}{p}\right) = \left(\frac{b}{p}\right)
$$

---

## Jacobi Symbol

For:
$$
n = p_1^{e_1} p_2^{e_2} \cdots p_k^{e_k}
$$

Define:

$$
\left(\frac{a}{n}\right) =
\left(\frac{a}{p_1}\right)^{e_1}
\left(\frac{a}{p_2}\right)^{e_2}
\cdots
\left(\frac{a}{p_k}\right)^{e_k}
$$

---

## Properties (Jacobi)

$$
\left(\frac{ab}{n}\right) = \left(\frac{a}{n}\right)\left(\frac{b}{n}\right)
$$

$$
\left(\frac{a}{mn}\right) = \left(\frac{a}{m}\right)\left(\frac{a}{n}\right)
$$

---

## Quadratic Reciprocity

$$
\left(\frac{m}{n}\right)\left(\frac{n}{m}\right) =
(-1)^{\frac{(m-1)(n-1)}{4}}
$$

---

## Key Insight

* Fast QR checking using:
  $$
  a^{\frac{p-1}{2}} \mod p
  $$

* Used in:

  * Primality testing
  * Cryptography

---

## Note

* Jacobi ≠ guarantee of QR
* Only:
  $$
  \left(\frac{a}{n}\right) = -1 \Rightarrow \text{non-QR}
  $$

---

## Use

* Efficient computation of residue properties
* Important in crypto algorithms

## Lecture 35: <br />Jacobi Symbol (Contd.)
:::

## Jacobi Symbol Computation

Given:

$$
\left(\frac{a}{n}\right), \quad n = p_1^{e_1} p_2^{e_2} \cdots p_k^{e_k}
$$

$$
\left(\frac{a}{n}\right) =
\left(\frac{a}{p_1}\right)^{e_1}
\left(\frac{a}{p_2}\right)^{e_2}
\cdots
\left(\frac{a}{p_k}\right)^{e_k}
$$

---

## Example Structure

For:

$$
n = 9975 = 3 \cdot 5^2 \cdot 7 \cdot 19
$$

$$
\left(\frac{6278}{9975}\right) =
\left(\frac{6278}{3}\right)
\left(\frac{6278}{5}\right)^2
\left(\frac{6278}{7}\right)
\left(\frac{6278}{19}\right)
$$

✔ Reduce using:

$$
a \equiv b \pmod{p}
$$

---

## Euler-Based Check

$$
\left(\frac{a}{p}\right) \equiv a^{\frac{p-1}{2}} \mod p
$$

---

## Euler Pseudoprime

If:

$$
\left(\frac{a}{n}\right) \equiv a^{\frac{n-1}{2}} \mod n
$$

Then:

* (n) behaves like prime for base (a)
* Called **Euler pseudoprime**

---

## Solovay–Strassen Test

### Input:

* Odd integer ( n > 2 )

### Steps:

1. Choose random:

$$
a \in [1, n-1]
$$

2. Compute:

$$
x = \left(\frac{a}{n}\right)
$$

3. Compute:

$$
y \equiv a^{\frac{n-1}{2}} \mod n
$$

---

### Decision:

* If:

$$
x = 0
$$

→ (n) is **composite**

---

* If:

$$
y \not\equiv x \mod n
$$

→ (n) is **composite**

---

* Else:

→ (n) is **probably prime**

---

## Property

* If algorithm says **composite → always correct**
* If **prime → probabilistic**

---

## Goldwasser–Micali Cryptosystem

### Key Generation

* Choose primes:

$$
p, q
$$

* Compute:

$$
n = pq
$$

* Choose:

$$
m \text{ such that }
\left(\frac{m}{p}\right) = -1,;
\left(\frac{m}{q}\right) = -1
$$

Then:

$$
\left(\frac{m}{n}\right) = 1
$$

✔ (m) = pseudo-quadratic residue

---

### Public Key

$$
(n, m)
$$

### Private Key

$$
(p, q)
$$

---

## Encryption

Plaintext:

$$
x \in {0,1}
$$

Choose random:

$$
r \in \mathbb{Z}_n^*
$$

Ciphertext:

$$
y \equiv r^2 \cdot m^x \mod n
$$

---

## Decryption

Check QR:

* If:

$$
y \text{ is QR mod } p \text{ and } q
$$

→

$$
x = 0
$$

---

* Else:

$$
x = 1
$$

---

## Key Insight

* Security based on:

  * Difficulty of distinguishing QR vs non-QR
  * Without knowing (p, q)

---

## Important Note

* Jacobi = 1 does NOT guarantee QR
* Used to hide bit information securely

---

## Use

* Probabilistic primality testing
* Bit encryption scheme (GM cryptosystem)
