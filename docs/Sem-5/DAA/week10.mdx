:::info[TITLE]
## Lecture 46: <br />Cryptoanalysis
:::

## Cryptology

* Two parts:

  * Cryptography (design)
  * Cryptanalysis (attack)

---

## Attack Models

### 1. Ciphertext-Only Attack (COA)

* Attacker knows:
  $$
  C
  $$

* Goal:

  * Recover:
    $$
    P \text{ or } K
    $$

---

### 2. Known-Plaintext Attack (KPA)

* Attacker knows:
  $$
  (P,; C)
  $$

* From previous communications

---

### 3. Chosen-Plaintext Attack (CPA)

* Attacker can choose:
  $$
  P_i
  $$

* Gets corresponding:
  $$
  C_i
  $$

* Goal:

  * Break encryption
  * Predict new plaintext

---

### 4. Chosen-Ciphertext Attack (CCA)

* Attacker chooses:
  $$
  C_i
  $$

* Gets:
  $$
  P_i
  $$

* Strongest attack model

---

## Attacker Goal

* Find:
  $$
  K
  $$

OR

* Given:
  $$
  C^*
  $$

Find:
$$
P^*
$$

---

## Generic Attacks

### 1. Exhaustive Search (Brute Force)

* Try all keys:
  $$
  K_1, K_2, ..., K_n
  $$

* Until correct one found

---

### 2. Table Lookup Attack

* Precompute table:

$$
(P,; C)
$$

* Store mappings

* On intercept:
  $$
  C \rightarrow \text{lookup } P
  $$

✔ Very fast at runtime

---

## One-Way Function

Function:
$$
f: A \rightarrow B
$$

---

### Properties

1. Easy to compute:
   $$
   y = f(x)
   $$

2. Hard to invert:
   $$
   x = f^{-1}(y)
   $$

---

## In Cryptography

* Encryption:
  $$
  C = E_K(P)
  $$

* Hard to recover:
  $$
  P \text{ or } K
  $$

---

## Table-Based Attack Idea

* Precompute:
  $$
  E_K(P)
  $$

for many keys

* Store results

* Match with intercepted:
  $$
  C
  $$

---

## Trade-off

* Time ↓ (fast lookup)
* Memory ↑ (large table)

---

## Key Insight

* Strong systems must resist:

  * CPA
  * CCA

✔ Security defined against strongest attacker

---

## Final Note

* Cryptanalysis evaluates:

  * Strength of system
  * Weakness under attack models

## Lecture 47: <br />Time- Memory Trade- off Attack
:::

## Time–Memory Trade-Off (TMTO)

---

## Goal

* Invert one-way function:
  $$
  y = f(x)
  $$

* Find:
  $$
  x
  $$

---

## Problem

* Brute force:

  * Time:
    $$
    O(N)
    $$
  * Memory:
    $$
    O(1)
    $$

* Table lookup:

  * Time:
    $$
    O(1)
    $$
  * Memory:
    $$
    O(N)
    $$

---

## Idea

* Balance:
  $$
  \text{Time} \leftrightarrow \text{Memory}
  $$

✔ Reduce both partially

---

## Hellman Method

---

## Precomputation Phase

* Choose chains of length:
  $$
  t
  $$

* Start with random:
  $$
  x_0
  $$

* Generate chain:

$$
x_1 = f(x_0),; x_2 = f(x_1),; ...,; x_t = f(x_{t-1})
$$

---

## Storage

* Store only:
  $$
  (\text{start},; \text{end})
  $$

* Not full chain

✔ Saves memory

---

## Table Size

* Number of chains:
  $$
  m
  $$

* Total covered space:
  $$
  \approx m \cdot t
  $$

---

## Online Phase (Attack)

Given:
$$
y = f(x)
$$

---

### Step 1: Check Endpoints

* Compare:
  $$
  y \text{ with stored endpoints}
  $$

---

### Step 2: If no match

* Compute:
  $$
  f(y),; f(f(y)),; ...
  $$

* Up to:
  $$
  t \text{ steps}
  $$

---

### Step 3: If match found

* Recompute chain from start
* Locate exact:
  $$
  x
  $$

---

## Maximum Work

$$
\le t \text{ operations}
$$

---

## Trade-off Relation

Let:

* (N) = key space

Then:

$$
T \cdot M^2 \approx N^2
$$

---

## Balanced Case

$$
T = M = N^{2/3}
$$

---

## Success Probability

* Depends on:
  $$
  \frac{m \cdot t}{N}
  $$

* More chains → higher success

---

## Key Insight

* Do heavy work **offline**
* Fast lookup **online**

---

## Limitation

* Chains may merge → collisions
* Reduces coverage

---

## Improvement

* Use multiple tables
* Different reduction functions

---

## Summary

* TMTO reduces:

  * Time from (O(N))
  * Memory from (O(N))

✔ Achieves practical balance for attacks

## Lecture 48: <br />Differential Cryptoanalysis
:::

## Differential Cryptanalysis

---

## Basic Idea

* Study how **input difference** affects **output difference**

---

## Definitions

* Input pair:
  $$
  x_1 = x,\quad x_2 = x \oplus \alpha
  $$

* Input difference:
  $$
  x_1 \oplus x_2 = \alpha
  $$

---

* Output:
  $$
  y_1 = F(x_1),\quad y_2 = F(x_2)
  $$

* Output difference:
  $$
  y_1 \oplus y_2 = \beta
  $$

---

## Differential

* Pair:
  $$
  (\alpha,; \beta)
  $$

* If:
  $$
  \alpha \rightarrow \beta \text{ with high probability}
  $$

✔ Useful for attack

---

## Attack Strategy

1. Choose many plaintext pairs:
   $$
   (x,; x \oplus \alpha)
   $$

2. Encrypt → get:
   $$
   (c_1,; c_2)
   $$

---

## Last Round Attack

* Guess subkey:
  $$
  k_r
  $$

---

### For each guess

* Partially decrypt:
  $$
  y_1 = F^{-1}(c_1,; k_r)
  $$

$$
y_2 = F^{-1}(c_2,; k_r)
$$

---

### Check condition

$$
y_1 \oplus y_2 = \beta
$$

---

### Count matches

* Maintain counter for each:
  $$
  k_r
  $$

✔ Correct key → highest count

---

## Key Space Example

* For DES:

$$
|K| = 2^{48} \text{ (last round)}
$$

---

## Finding Good Differentials

* Use **S-box analysis**

---

## Difference Distribution Table (DDT)

For each:
$$
\alpha
$$

* Compute all:
  $$
  x,; x \oplus \alpha
  $$

* Evaluate:
  $$
  \beta = S(x) \oplus S(x \oplus \alpha)
  $$

* Count frequency

---

## Goal

* Find:
  $$
  (\alpha,\beta) \text{ with high probability}
  $$

---

## Example (4-bit S-box)

* Choose:
  $$
  \alpha = 4
  $$

* For all inputs:

  * Compute output differences
  * Build frequency table

✔ Highest frequency → best differential

---

## Multi-Round Extension

* Combine round-wise differentials
* Multiply probabilities

---

## Key Insight

* Exploits **non-random behavior** of cipher
* Works well on:

  * SPN
  * Feistel structures

---

## Summary

* Input difference → predictable output difference
* Use statistics to recover key

✔ Powerful non-generic attack on block ciphers

## Lecture 49: <br />More on Differential Cryptoanalysis
:::

## Differential Attack (r-round)

---

### Step 1: Find Differential

* Find high-probability:
  $$
  \alpha \rightarrow \beta \text{ for } (r-1) \text{ rounds}
  $$

---

### Step 2: Initialize

* For all subkeys:
  $$
  K_r
  $$

* Set counter:
  $$
  \text{count}(K_r) = 0
  $$

---

### Step 3: Chosen Plaintexts

* Select:
  $$
  P_1,; P_2 \text{ such that } P_1 \oplus P_2 = \alpha
  $$

* Encrypt:
  $$
  C_1,; C_2
  $$

---

### Step 4: Partial Decryption

* For each guess:
  $$
  K_r
  $$

* Compute:
  $$
  y_1 = F^{-1}(C_1,; K_r)
  $$

$$
y_2 = F^{-1}(C_2,; K_r)
$$

---

### Step 5: Check

* If:
  $$
  y_1 \oplus y_2 = \beta
  $$

* Then:
  $$
  \text{count}(K_r)++
  $$

---

### Step 6: Repeat

* Until one key has significantly high count

✔ That is likely correct subkey

---

## Impossible Differential Attack

* Instead of high probability:

$$
\alpha \nrightarrow \beta \quad (\text{impossible})
$$

---

### Idea

* If a key produces:
  $$
  \beta
  $$

→ Reject that key

✔ Eliminate wrong keys

---

## Higher-Order Differential

* Use multiple differences

Example:

* Second-order:
  $$
  \Delta^2 x
  $$

✔ Captures complex patterns

---

## Differential Composition

Given:

$$
\alpha \rightarrow \beta \text{ with prob } p
$$

$$
\beta \rightarrow \delta \text{ with prob } q
$$

---

### Combined

$$
\alpha \rightarrow \delta \text{ with prob } p \cdot q
$$

---

## Attack Using Composition

### Step 1

Choose:
$$
P_1,; P_2 \text{ with } P_1 \oplus P_2 = \alpha
$$

---

### Step 2

Get:
$$
C_1,; C_2
$$

Construct:
$$
C_3 = C_1 \oplus \delta,\quad C_4 = C_2 \oplus \delta
$$

---

### Step 3

Decrypt:
$$
P_3,; P_4
$$

---

### Step 4

Check:
$$
P_3 \oplus P_4 = \alpha
$$

---

### Step 5

Repeat → count valid patterns

---

## Key Insight

* Break cipher by:

  * Tracking differences
  * Using probability bias

---

## Applications

* Effective on:

  * DES (reduced rounds)
  * SPN ciphers

---

## Summary

* Uses:
  $$
  \text{input difference} \rightarrow \text{output difference}
  $$

* Types:

  * Standard differential
  * Impossible differential
  * Higher-order differential

✔ Powerful statistical cryptanalysis technique

## Lecture 50: <br />Linear Cryptoanalysis
:::


## Attack Model

* Uses:

  * Known plaintext pairs:
    $$
    (P_i\ , C_i)
    $$

* Goal:
  $$
  \text{Recover key } K
  $$

---

## Idea

* Approximate cipher using **linear relations**

---

## Linear Approximation

For bits:

$$
a_1 p_1 \oplus a_2 p_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus b_1 k_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus c_1 c_1 \oplus \cdots = 0
$$

* Holds with probability:
  $$
  p > \frac{1}{2}
  $$

---

## Bias

Define:

$$
\epsilon = p - \frac{1}{2}
$$

* If:
  $$
  \epsilon \ne 0
  $$

✔ Useful relation

---

## Boolean Function

* Input:
  $$
  (s_1, s_2, ..., s_n)
  $$

* Output:
  $$
  {0,1}
  $$

* Linear form:
  $$
  \bigoplus a_i s_i
  $$

---

## Combining Approximations (Piling-Up Lemma)

Let biases:

$$
\epsilon_1,; \epsilon_2,; ...,; \epsilon_k
$$

---

### Combined Bias

$$
\epsilon = 2^{k-1} \prod_{j=1}^{k} \epsilon_j
$$

---

### Combined Probability

$$
P = \frac{1}{2} + \epsilon
$$

---

## Special Case (2 variables)

$$
\epsilon_{i,j} = 2 \epsilon_i \epsilon_j
$$

---

## Key Observation

* If any:
  $$
  \epsilon_j = 0
  $$

Then:

$$
\epsilon = 0
$$

❌ No useful information

---

## Attack Steps

### Step 1: Find Linear Approximation

* For S-box / round function

---

### Step 2: Collect Data

* Gather many:
  $$
  (P,; C)
  $$

---

### Step 3: Evaluate Expression

* Compute LHS for all pairs

---

### Step 4: Count Bias

* Count how often:
  $$
  \text{expression} = 0
  $$

---

### Step 5: Guess Key Bits

* Try key candidates
* Select one with highest bias

✔ Likely correct key

---

## Application

* Applied to:

  * DES (Eurocrypt 1993)

* Needs:

  * Large number of plaintext-ciphertext pairs

---

## Key Insight

* Cipher ≠ perfectly random
* Linear patterns exist

---

## Summary

* Uses:
  $$
  \text{linear relation between } P, C, K
  $$

* Exploits:
  $$
  \text{bias } (\epsilon)
  $$

✔ Powerful statistical attack on block ciphers
